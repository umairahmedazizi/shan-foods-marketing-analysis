# Shan Foods: Brand and Marketing Analysis

A marketing case study of Shan Foods, one of Pakistan's best-known spice and recipe-mix brands.
The report looks at how Shan builds its brand and competes at home and abroad, and it draws on a
first-hand interview with the company's Manager of International Marketing.

This was a marketing coursework report.

## What it covers

The report follows the brand through the standard marketing lens, mixing company background with
the manager's own account and an analysis of the marketing mix.

- Company and brand background: vision, mission, certifications, and why we chose to study Shan.
- Product portfolio: the Shan and Shoop brands.
- Brand positioning: where Shan sits in the market, its target customers, and what it stands for.
- The competitive environment it operates in.
- The four Ps:
  - Price: premium pricing abroad, holding the line on prices during inflation, and a tiered
    pricing structure.
  - Place: how distributors are chosen, the role they play, and the push into international retail.
  - Promotion: digital marketing, influencer campaigns, and in-store taste trials aimed at
    younger buyers.

## A few takeaways

- Shan leans on trust and authenticity rather than low prices, which lets it charge a premium in
  export markets.
- Its distributor network is what makes that international reach possible while keeping the brand
  consistent.
- Promotion is increasingly digital and experiential, with sampling used to win over newer
  customers.
